FAQ

Your Questions
Answered About Our
Cooperative Banking

Bandhan Multi State Cooperative Credit Society Ltd. is a registered cooperative entity under the Multi-State Co-Operative Societies Act, 2002.

What is a Multi-State Cooperative Credit Society?
A Multi-State Cooperative Credit Society (MSCCS) is a registered cooperative entity that operates across multiple states in India. We function similar to a credit union, promoting thrift and savings among members while providing financial support through loans and deposits. We are governed by the Central Registrar of Cooperative Societies (CRCS) under the Multi-State Co-Operative Societies Act, 2002.
How is Bandhan MSCC different from a regular bank?
Bandhan Multi State Cooperative Credit Society operates on cooperative principles — we are member-owned and member-focused. Unlike traditional banks, we work for the benefit of our members. Our governance is democratic, with members having a say in decision-making, ensuring transparency and accountability in all operations.
Who can become a member of Bandhan MSCC?
Any individual who meets our membership criteria can join Bandhan Multi State Cooperative Credit Society. Membership is open to residents across multiple states where we operate. To become a member, fill out a membership application, provide identification documents, and purchase a minimum share capital as per our bylaws.
Are my deposits safe with Bandhan MSCC?
Yes, absolutely. We are registered under the Multi-State Co-Operative Societies Act, 2002 and regulated by the Central Registrar. We maintain strict financial discipline and transparency in all operations to ensure the safety of members' deposits.
